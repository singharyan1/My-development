The Glory of Sonepur

Situated at the confluence of the sacred rivers Ganges and Gandak, Sonepur is a city steeped in profound spiritual and historical significance. It is not merely a geographic location; it is the heartbeat of Bihar's rich cultural tapestry.

For centuries, it has been a beacon of trade, spirituality, and unity. Today, Sonepur stands on the cusp of a modern renaissance, blending its timeless legacy with futuristic growth.

The Great Sonepur Mela

Witness the spectacle of Asia's largest cattle fair. The Sonepur Mela is a breathtaking display of commerce and culture, dating back to ancient times when Emperor Chandragupta Maurya used it to buy elephants and horses across the river Ganges.

From decorated elephants to vibrant folk performances, the Mela is a living museum of Indian tradition that attracts millions of visitors from across the globe every year in November-December.

Harihar Nath Mandir

At the heart of Sonepur lies the magnificent Harihar Nath Mandir, uniquely dedicated to the divine union of Lord Vishnu (Hari) and Lord Shiva (Har). According to legend, the temple was originally built by Lord Rama on his way to King Janaka's court.

The temple is the epicenter of spiritual energy, where devotees gather to bathe at the sacred confluence on Kartik Purnima before seeking blessings. The architectural beauty and spiritual aura make it a monumental heritage site.
